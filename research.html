research

I work at the intersection of genomics and machine learning. Below are the academic projects I've worked on, most recent first.

Virtual cells with latent reasoning

Boeva Lab, ETH Zürich · Oct 2025 – present

I am exploring recurrent architectures that maintain a latent reasoning state to predict cellular responses under interventions.

Alongside, I am benchmarking state-of-the-art virtual cell models on cellular perturbation-response prediction to understand where current architectures actually succeed and where they over-generalize.

ICML 2026 poster: benchmarking virtual cell models

DNA shape and its effect on gene expression

Steve Kelly Lab, University of Oxford · Sep 2024 – Jun 2025 · Master's thesis

I engineered a convolutional neural network that predicts promoter location and its effect on gene expression purely from the physical shape of DNA.

FiberFold: predicting 3D chromatin organization from single-molecule sequencing

Altemose Lab, Stanford · Jun – Sep 2024

I worked with Danilo Dubocanin on preprocesing and training pipeline for FiberFold to predict Hi-C chromatin contact maps from single-molecule Fiber-seq data (long-read methylation footprints). Fiber-seq captures haplotype-resolved chromatin state on individual DNA molecules, and FiberFold turns that signal into predicted 3D contact maps without needing population-level Hi-C input. We applied this to patient-derived samples with chromosomal translocations.

preprint: Dubocanin D., Kalygina A., et al. Integrating single-molecule sequencing and deep learning to predict haplotype-specific 3D chromatin organization in a Mendelian condition. bioRxiv, 2025. doi:10.1101/2025.02.26.640261 · code

Recycling protein pathways at the membrane

Kovtun Lab, Max Planck Institute for Multidisciplinary Sciences · Jul – Sep 2022

I worked in a structural biology lab studying transmembrane trafficking proteins. On the computational side, I implemented a sliding-window algorithm to analyze particle colocalization on electron microscopy images, and wrote macros to simulate large protein interactions using AlphaFold.

On the wet side, I contributed to preparation for the cryo-EM experiments aimed at understanding how recycling protein pathways are organized at the membrane.
